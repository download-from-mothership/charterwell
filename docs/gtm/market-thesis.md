# Charterwell Market Thesis

## 1. The Global AI-in-Insurance Market

The global AI-in-insurance market is valued at approximately **$13.5 billion in 2026**, growing at a **35.7% CAGR** to **$154+ billion by 2034** (Fortune Business Insights). Alternative estimates range higher — Precedence Research projects $176.6B by 2035 at 32.2% CAGR, and Market Research Future estimates $303.3B by 2035 at 32.3% CAGR. The variance reflects different scoping methodologies, but all sources agree on the directional thesis: this is a market that will 10x+ in under a decade.

**North America leads** with ~40% share in 2025. The underwriting segment grows fastest (41.6% CAGR) due to AI-driven risk models, but claims processing — Charterwell's entry point — is the largest operational cost center and the most document-intensive workflow.

### Where Charterwell Fits in the Value Chain

Charterwell enters at the **document layer** — the substrate beneath every insurance workflow. Underwriting, claims, compliance, and policy servicing all depend on extracting structured data from unstructured documents (PDFs, images, handwritten forms, medical records, police reports). Rather than competing in a single vertical (just fraud, just underwriting), Charterwell builds the **horizontal document intelligence platform** that powers all of them.

This is analogous to how Palantir built a data integration layer for government before expanding to commercial — the document layer is the wedge, the workspace is the moat.

## 2. AI in Insurance Claims Processing

The AI-in-insurance-claims-processing market is valued at **$514 million in 2024**, projected to reach **$2.76 billion by 2034** at an **18.3% CAGR** (Market.us).

### Regional Breakdown
- **North America**: 37.3% share ($191.8M in 2024)
- **U.S. specifically**: $172.65M in 2024, 17.7% CAGR
- **Software segment**: 78.6% of market (vs. services)
- **Life & Health**: 58.4% share today, but P&C is catching up fast

### Pain Points Driving Growth

| Pain Point | Impact | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Manual document triage** | Examiners spend 15-20 min per FNOL validating data, cross-referencing policies, flagging inconsistencies. Complex commercial claims take hours. | Millions of claims/year across the industry |
| **Adjuster bottlenecks** | Experienced adjusters are retiring. New adjusters lack coverage depth. Misallocation of adjusters to claims increases cycle times and costs. | Adjuster workforce declining while claim volumes rise |
| **Fraud detection lag** | Manual review catches fraud late or not at all. AI-assisted fraud detection is emerging but rarely integrated into document workflows. | $80B+ annual insurance fraud in the U.S. alone |
| **Compliance overhead** | Regulatory requirements (state-by-state in U.S.) create documentation burden. Manual compliance checks are error-prone. | 50-state regulatory landscape, increasing scrutiny |
| **Document chaos** | Paper-based filing and scattered digital repositories make it hard to find information. No single source of truth for a claim file. | Average P&C claim touches 10-15 documents |

### The Adoption Gap

**88% of auto insurers** and **70% of home insurers** report using or planning to use AI — but only **7% have successfully scaled AI into production**. This gap between intent and execution is Charterwell's opportunity. Carriers want this. They just can't build it themselves.

Industry benchmarks show **15-30% cost reductions** and **30-50% cycle-time improvements** from claims automation. The ROI case is proven; the implementation is the bottleneck.

## 3. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

### Total Addressable Market (TAM): $40.8B+

The **global claims processing software market** is estimated at **$40.84 billion in 2024**, growing at 8.3% CAGR. This represents the full market for any software involved in processing insurance claims — legacy systems, modern SaaS, and AI-native platforms.

The broader context:
- **Global P&C insurance market**: $4.3 trillion in premiums (2025)
- **U.S. P&C premiums**: ~$1.14 trillion (2026)
- **Global IDP market**: $3.2-10.6B in 2025 (depending on scope), growing 33.7% CAGR to $44-91B by 2034
- **BFSI share of IDP**: ~39% (~$1.3-4.1B attributable to insurance/financial services)

Charterwell's TAM is the intersection of claims processing software and intelligent document processing for insurance: **~$40B in total claims software spend** + **~$4B in insurance-adjacent IDP spend** = **$40-45B TAM**.

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $6-8B

Narrowing to the segments Charterwell can realistically serve:

- **AI-specific claims processing**: $2.76B by 2034 (growing from $514M today)
- **P&C insurance software**: $18.3B in 2023, growing to $26.4B by 2030
- **Insurance IDP** (BFSI segment × insurance share): ~$1.5-2B today, growing rapidly

Charterwell's SAM focuses on:
1. **P&C carriers** first (50% of U.S. premiums, ~$570B)
2. **Mid-market and large carriers** with enough volume to justify AI but not enough engineering talent to build in-house
3. **Document-intensive workflows**: claims FNOL, damage assessment, subrogation, SIU/fraud
4. **U.S. and English-speaking markets** initially

Estimated SAM: **$6-8B** (P&C claims AI + document processing for target segments).

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): $150-300M in Year 5

Realistic capture in the first 3-5 years:

- **Year 1-2**: 5-10 design partners, $1-3M ARR. Prove the product on P&C claims document triage.
- **Year 3**: 30-50 carrier customers, $10-25M ARR. Expand from claims to underwriting document processing.
- **Year 5**: 100+ customers across P&C, specialty, and early life/health. $150-300M ARR.

Assumptions:
- Average contract value: $500K-2M/year for mid-market carriers, $5-10M for top-20 carriers
- P&C claims is the beachhead; underwriting and compliance are natural expansions
- Land with one workflow (FNOL document triage), expand to full claims workspace

SOM represents **~2-5% of SAM** at maturity — conservative for a category-defining platform but realistic given enterprise sales cycles in insurance.

## 5. Key Thesis Takeaways

1. **The market is real and large.** $13.5B in AI-in-insurance spend today, growing to $154B+ by 2034. Claims processing alone is a $2.76B AI market by 2034.

2. **The pain is acute and quantified.** 15-20 min per FNOL, 30-50% cycle time reduction possible, $80B+ in annual fraud. Carriers know they need AI — 88% say so — but only 7% have scaled it.

3. **The gap is execution, not intent.** This is a build-vs-buy market tipping toward buy. Carriers lack AI engineering talent. Point solutions solve one problem. No one owns the document layer.

4. **Charterwell's wedge is the document workspace.** Not a point solution for fraud or a chatbot for customer service. A full-stack, vertical AI workspace for insurance professionals — starting with document intelligence for P&C claims.

5. **The TAM supports a large outcome.** $40B+ in claims software, $6-8B SAM for AI document processing in P&C, $150-300M SOM in 5 years. This is a venture-scale opportunity with a clear expansion path to $1B+ ARR.
