## The Problem

Insurance runs on documents. Every claim starts with a stack of unstructured paper: police reports, medical records, policy declarations, damage photos, repair estimates, adjuster notes, compliance filings. A single P&C claim touches 10-15 documents on average. Complex commercial claims touch hundreds.

Today, claims adjusters spend 15-20 minutes per first notice of loss just validating data, cross-referencing policies, and flagging inconsistencies. Experienced adjusters — the ones who actually understand coverage nuances — are retiring. New adjusters lack the depth. The entire industry is losing its institutional knowledge at the same time claim volumes are rising.

The result: **$80 billion in annual insurance fraud in the U.S. alone**, slow claim resolution, rising operational costs, and a compliance burden that grows every year across 50 different state regulatory regimes.

**88% of insurers say they are deploying or planning to deploy AI. Only 7% have successfully scaled it into production.** The gap between intent and execution is enormous. Carriers know they need this. They cannot build it themselves.

## The Landscape — And the Gap

We have profiled every material competitor. Here is what we found:

**Point solutions** solve one problem well but force carriers to stitch together 5-10 vendors:
- Tractable ($185M raised, $1B valuation) does computer vision for damage photos. Narrow: photos only.
- Shift Technology ($320M raised, $1B valuation) does fraud detection. Expanding into claims triage but lacks a document intelligence layer.
- Sprout.ai ($38M raised) automates claim workflows. MetLife just expanded their partnership globally. Validates demand, but Sprout automates the process *around* documents without deeply understanding the documents themselves.

**Horizontal IDP platforms** extract data but don't understand insurance:
- Hyperscience ($300M+ raised) delivers 99.5% extraction accuracy but has no concept of coverage types, state-specific compliance, or adjudication guidelines. It is a tool, not a workspace.

**Legacy incumbents** manage records but don't process unstructured content:
- Guidewire ($1.2B revenue), Duck Creek, and Majesco are the systems of record. They manage claim records — they do not *understand* claim documents. Together they hold ~16% of the $15B insurance software market. They are infrastructure. We are intelligence.

**One emerging competitor** is building toward a similar vision:
- FurtherAI ($30M raised, Andreessen Horowitz) is the closest analog — a broad AI assistant for insurance workflows. But they are going wide across many workflow types rather than going deep on the document layer. Breadth without depth is vulnerable.

**The gap: no one has built the full-stack, vertical AI workspace for insurance document workflows.** Point solutions are narrow. Horizontal platforms are generic. Incumbents are slow. The workspace — the operating system — does not exist yet.

## The Product

Charterwell is the operating system for insurance document workflows.

**Intake → Extraction → Routing → Adjudication → Compliance → Payout.**

Every feature starts with understanding unstructured documents — PDFs, images, handwritten forms, medical records, police reports, policy declarations. Not generic extraction. Insurance-intelligent extraction that understands coverage types, regulatory requirements, and carrier-specific adjudication rules.

### How It Works

1. **Intake.** Documents arrive in any format — email attachments, fax (yes, still), uploaded images, EDI feeds. Charterwell normalizes and classifies them automatically.

2. **Extraction.** AI extracts structured data from every document type: loss details, policy numbers, coverage limits, medical codes, repair estimates, witness statements. Not just OCR — contextual understanding of what the data *means* for this specific claim.

3. **Routing.** The claim is automatically routed to the right adjuster based on coverage type, complexity, and carrier-specific rules. Simple claims are fast-tracked. Complex claims get the experienced adjusters.

4. **Adjudication.** AI-assisted decision support: does this claim fall within coverage? Are there exclusions? What is the reserve estimate? What are the comparable precedents? The adjuster stays in control; the AI eliminates the research time.

5. **Compliance.** State-specific filing requirements, NAIC standards, fraud indicators, SIU referral triggers — all checked automatically, all documented for audit trail.

6. **Payout.** Approved claims flow directly to payment systems. The entire lifecycle — from first notice of loss to payment — lives in one workspace.

### The Playbook System

This is where Charterwell becomes irreplaceable. Carriers codify their own adjudication guidelines, escalation rules, and compliance checklists into reusable, AI-enforced **playbooks**. Every carrier's playbooks are different — reflecting their specific risk appetite, regulatory posture, and claims philosophy.

Once a carrier has invested in building their playbooks inside Charterwell, switching costs are enormous. This is not vendor lock-in through contract terms. It is lock-in through value creation — the carrier's institutional knowledge *lives* in Charterwell.

## Why Now

Three forces are converging:

1. **The AI capability threshold has been crossed.** Large language models and vision models can now process insurance documents with the accuracy and nuance that the industry demands. This was not true two years ago.

2. **The workforce crisis is accelerating.** Experienced adjusters are retiring. New adjusters take years to train. AI is no longer optional — it is the only way to maintain quality as headcount declines.

3. **Regulatory pressure is increasing.** State insurance departments are demanding faster claim resolution, better fraud detection, and more rigorous compliance documentation. Manual processes cannot keep up.
