## Conflict Validation Summary

### Conflict 1: Chartwell Insurance / EPIC — HIGH (Unchanged)

**Status confirmed.** EPIC acquired Chartwell Insurance Services on February 10, 2025. The Chartwell brand operates as a sub-brand within EPIC's private client practice (Chicago). chartwellins.com redirects to epicbrokers.com.

**Key finding:** No standalone "CHARTWELL" trademark filing by EPIC was identified in 2025-2026. No TTAB opposition proceedings involving any "Chartwell" mark were found.

**Risk trajectory:** Stable. EPIC has the resources to oppose but has not taken proactive trademark action. The Chartwell brand is being absorbed into EPIC's identity, which may weaken standalone common-law rights over time — but EPIC could reverse course at any point.

**Action:** Continue monthly monitoring for new EPIC trademark filings.

**Brand sunset confirmed.** Live verification shows:

**Risk trajectory:** Declining. The brand is in late-stage sunset. Common-law rights are actively eroding. At current trajectory, residual rights will be insufficient to sustain opposition by mid-2027.

**Action:** Quarterly monitoring. This conflict is trending toward LOW within 12-18 months.

### Conflict 3: ChartWellAI — MODERATE-HIGH (Upgraded from MODERATE)

**Registration confirmed.** Both marks have matured from pending to registered:
- **CHARTWELL AI** (Serial No. 99558440) — Registered, Class 42
- **CHARTWELLAI** (Serial No. 99558514) — Registered, Class 42

Goods/services confirmed as exclusively healthcare-focused: clinical notes, EHR integration, clinician decision-making support, diagnostic workflow automation, medical education, clinician well-being tools.

**Risk trajectory:** Stable at MODERATE-HIGH. These are now citable registered marks. A Section 2(d) Office Action citing one or both registrations is a near-certainty for a CHARTERWELL Class 42 filing.

**Critical action:** Pre-draft the Office Action response brief **before** filing. Key arguments:
1. Different industry verticals (healthcare AI vs. insurance/legal document processing)
2. Different end users (clinicians vs. insurance professionals and attorneys)
3. Narrow goods/services descriptions on both sides
4. Crowded field of CHARTWELL marks diluting Class 42 exclusivity

**Budget impact:** $3,000-$7,000 for Office Action response (treat as a near-certainty, not a contingency).
