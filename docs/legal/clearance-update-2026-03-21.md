## 2. Material Updates

### 2.1 ELEVATED: ChartWellAI Domain Confusion Risk (NEW ISSUE)

**Finding:** ChartWellAI, Inc. operates its healthcare AI platform at **chartwell.ai**. Charterwell's intended domain is **charterwell.ai**.

**Why this matters:**
- The domains differ by a single letter ("r"): `charterwell.ai` vs. `chartwell.ai`
- Users who drop the "r" when typing the URL will land on ChartWellAI's healthcare platform — creating actual consumer confusion
- This is the same phonetic confusion problem identified for the marks generally, but amplified in the digital channel where typos and autocomplete are common
- A USPTO examining attorney — or ChartWellAI's counsel — could point to the domain similarity as evidence of likely confusion in the marketplace
- ChartWellAI is actively operating and generating traffic; this is not a parked domain

**Risk upgrade:** ChartWellAI conflict moves from **MODERATE** to **MODERATE-HIGH** when domain-level confusion is factored in. The marks are in different industries (healthcare vs. insurance), which remains a strong differentiator, but the domain proximity creates a concrete, demonstrable confusion vector.

**Recommended mitigation:**
1. Attempt to acquire **chartwell.ai** — unlikely to succeed (active business), but worth a broker inquiry to establish diligence
2. If acquisition fails, document the attempt. This shows good faith and awareness of the conflict
3. Invest heavily in branded search terms and SEO from day one to ensure "charterwell" searches resolve correctly
4. Consider registering **charterwell.com** as the primary commercial domain, using **.ai** as a secondary/vanity domain. Enterprise insurance buyers expect **.com** — this also creates distance from ChartWellAI's **.ai** domain
5. In the USPTO filing, emphasize the insurance-specific nature of goods/services to maximize distance from ChartWellAI's healthcare description

### 2.2 UPGRADED: Chartwell Compliance Brand Persistence

**Finding:** The initial opinion assessed Chartwell Compliance as "transitioning away" from the Chartwell brand after the March 2023 Ankura acquisition. Live research shows the Chartwell Compliance brand is **more persistent than expected**:

- chartwellcompliance.com remains fully operational (not just a redirect)
- LinkedIn page active with 66 employees listed (as of July 2024)
- Brand appears as "Chartwell Compliance" within Ankura's compliance practice, not fully absorbed
- Media and industry references continue to use "Chartwell Compliance" as a recognizable brand

**Revised assessment:** Upgrade from LOW-MODERATE to **MODERATE**. The Chartwell Compliance brand may persist indefinitely as a practice-area brand within Ankura, similar to how consulting firms maintain acquired brand names. This means:

- Common-law rights in the "Chartwell" name for compliance/fintech services are not diminishing as fast as projected
- If Charterwell's product expands into compliance-adjacent features (regulatory document processing, compliance checking), the overlap increases
- Ankura is a large firm (1,800+ employees) with legal resources to monitor and oppose if they perceive encroachment

**Recommended mitigation:**
1. Monitor Chartwell Compliance / Ankura for any trademark filing activity
2. In marketing materials and goods/services descriptions, maintain clear separation between "document processing for claims" and "compliance advisory services"
3. If Charterwell adds compliance-checking features, describe them as software tools, not compliance services — the distinction matters for trademark purposes

## 5. Regulatory Note: December 2025 TMEP Update

The USPTO published an updated Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP) in December 2025, effective for 2026 filings. Key changes relevant to Charterwell:

- **Domicile address requirements tightened** — Virtual offices and mail forwarding services may not qualify. Ensure the filing uses a legitimate business address for the applicant entity.
- **Specimen standards refined** — Specimens of use must reflect actual use in commerce. For intent-to-use filings, this applies at the Statement of Use stage, not initial filing.
- **Fraud prevention measures enhanced** — Increased scrutiny on specimen submissions.

**Impact on Charterwell:** Minimal for the initial 1(b) filing, but the company must have a bona fide intent to use the mark in commerce and must provide a real domicile address. Ensure the corporate entity is formed (or in process) before filing.
