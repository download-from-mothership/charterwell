# Charterwell Go-to-Market Motion

**Prepared by:** CMO | **Date:** 2026-03-21 | **Issue:** KEN-39

---

## Executive Summary

This document designs Charterwell's go-to-market strategy for the first 12 months. The motion follows the enterprise playbook for vertical AI: **design partners → proof of value → founder-led sales → channel expansion.** Insurance has 6-12 month sales cycles, conservative buyers, and a strong "show me it works on my data" culture. Our GTM must respect these realities while moving faster than Shift Technology's expansion into claims automation and FurtherAI's a16z-backed growth.

---

## 1. Design Partner Acquisition (Months 1-4)

### Target Profile

The ideal design partner is a mid-market P&C carrier that:

| Criterion | Specification | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Size** | $1B-$10B gross written premiums | Large enough to have real document volume; small enough to move fast. Top-20 carriers (State Farm, Allstate, Progressive) are slow — save for Year 2. |
| **Claims volume** | 50K-500K claims/year | Enough volume to train models and prove ROI. Not so much that pilot scope becomes unmanageable. |
| **AI posture** | Active AI initiatives or recently hired Chief AI Officer / Chief Digital Officer | Signals budget allocation and leadership buy-in for AI. Eliminates carriers still in "wait and see" mode. |
| **Core system** | Guidewire or Duck Creek | Integration with the dominant platforms maximizes replicability of the pilot to other carriers. |
| **Pain point** | Documented frustration with claims cycle time, document processing, or tool fragmentation | The design partner must feel the pain today, not theoretically. |

### Prospect Research: Tiered Target List

**Tier 1 — High Priority (active AI transformation, public signals):**

| Signal to Research | Where to Find It | What It Tells Us |
|---|---|---|
| Chief AI Officer / Chief Digital Officer hired in last 18 months | LinkedIn, press releases, industry publications | Leadership mandate for AI adoption. Budget is allocated. |
| Public AI transformation initiative | Carrier annual reports, press releases, conference presentations | Board-level commitment. Not just an innovation lab experiment. |
| Innovation lab or accelerator program | Carrier websites, InsureTech Connect alumni lists, Plug and Play participants | Open to startup partnerships. Familiar with pilot-to-production path. |
| Recent Guidewire Cloud migration | Guidewire press releases, conference presentations | Modern infrastructure. Ready for API-based integrations. |
| Public statements about claims AI challenges | Earnings calls, industry conference presentations, Insurance Journal quotes | Self-identified pain. The prospect has already articulated the problem we solve. |

**Tier 2 — Medium Priority (likely pain, less public signal):**
- Carriers with high combined ratios (>100%) — under pressure to reduce claims costs
- Carriers in catastrophe-heavy states (FL, TX, CA) — surge workload pain is acute
- Carriers that recently expanded into new lines of business — document complexity is increasing

**Tier 3 — Opportunistic:**
- Carriers referred by investors, advisors, or conference contacts
- Carriers that respond to thought leadership content (whitepaper downloads, conference conversations)

### Design Partner Value Exchange

| What They Get | What We Get |
|---|---|
| Free or deeply discounted access to Charterwell for 6-12 months | Real claims documents to train and validate models |
| Co-development influence — their pain points shape the product | Production deployment reference customer |
| Priority support and direct access to founding team | Case study rights (anonymized if needed) |
| Early adopter pricing locked in for enterprise conversion | Feedback loop on product-market fit |
| Competitive advantage: first carrier in their peer group with an AI Claims Workspace | Revenue: conversion to paid at end of pilot |

### Outreach Strategy

| Channel | Approach | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm intros (investors, advisors)** | Highest conversion. Ask every investor and advisor for 3-5 carrier introductions. | 15-25 intros |
| **Conference networking** | ITC Vegas (Sep 29-Oct 1, 2026) is the #1 target event. Also: InsureTech Connect, CLM Annual Conference. | 10-20 conversations |
| **LinkedIn direct outreach** | Target VP Claims and Chief Digital Officers at Tier 1 carriers. Personalized messages referencing their specific AI initiatives. | 50-100 messages |
| **Thought leadership inbound** | Manifesto whitepaper → landing page → email capture → nurture → conversation. | Ongoing funnel |
| **Industry analyst introductions** | Brief Celent and Novarica. Ask them which carriers are actively evaluating claims AI. | 2-3 analyst briefings |

**Target outcome:** 10 qualified conversations → 5 pilots → 2-3 paid conversions within 12 months.

## 2. Proof of Value Framework (Months 4-8)

### Pilot Structure

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 60-90 days |
| **Scope** | One claim type (e.g., auto physical damage, water damage, slip-and-fall) in one geography |
| **Volume** | 500-2,000 claims during pilot period |
| **Integration** | API connection to carrier's CMS (Guidewire/Duck Creek) or standalone upload for initial phase |
| **Success criteria** | Defined collaboratively with VP Claims before pilot starts |

### Success Metrics

These are the numbers a VP Claims needs to see to convert from pilot to paid:

| Metric | Target | How We Measure |
|---|---|---|
| **Document triage time reduction** | ≥50% reduction in time from document receipt to structured data available | Time-stamped comparison: current manual process vs. Charterwell-assisted process |
| **Extraction accuracy** | ≥95% accuracy on key fields (policy numbers, loss dates, coverage types, amounts) | Human audit of random sample (100+ claims) |
| **Claims cycle time impact** | ≥20% reduction in average cycle time for pilot claim type | Before/after comparison on matched claim cohorts |
| **Adjuster productivity** | ≥30% increase in claims processed per adjuster per day | Throughput measurement during pilot period |
| **Error rate reduction** | ≥40% reduction in data entry errors and missed fields | Error audit comparing manual vs. Charterwell-assisted claims |
| **User satisfaction** | ≥4.0/5.0 from claims professionals using the workspace | Post-pilot survey |

### Pilot-to-Enterprise Conversion Path

```
Pilot (60-90 days, 1 claim type, 1 geography)
        ↓ Success criteria met
Phase 1 expansion (additional claim types, same geography)
        ↓ Validated across claim types
Phase 2 expansion (all geographies, all P&C claim types)
        ↓ Full deployment
Enterprise contract (3-5 year term)
```

### What "Not Ready" Looks Like

Be honest about pilot disqualification. If any of these are true, delay the pilot:
- Carrier has no Guidewire/Duck Creek (integration cost too high for pilot)
- Carrier cannot provide sample documents for pre-pilot testing
- No executive sponsor — team lead interest without VP Claims buy-in
- Carrier is mid-core-system migration (too much change happening)

## 3. Sales Motion

### Phase 1: Founder-Led Sales (Months 1-12)

Insurance enterprise sales requires trust, domain credibility, and persistence. In Year 1, every deal is founder-led:

| Activity | Cadence | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| **Prospect discovery calls** | 3-5 per week | CEO / CMO |
| **Product demos (tailored to carrier's documents)** | 2-3 per week | CEO + Product |
| **Pilot kickoff and management** | Ongoing (manage 3-5 concurrent pilots) | CEO + Engineering |
| **Quarterly business reviews with design partners** | Every 90 days | CEO |
| **Conference attendance and speaking** | 3-5 events per year | CEO / CMO |

### Phase 2: Repeatable Sales Motion (Months 12-18)

Once 2-3 enterprise contracts are signed, begin building the repeatable motion:

| Hire | Timing | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **VP Sales** | Month 10-12 | Build sales playbook from founder-led deals. Hire 2-3 AEs. |
| **Solutions Engineer** | Month 8-10 | Own technical demos, pilot deployments, and integration support. |
| **Customer Success Manager** | Month 10-12 | Own post-deployment expansion and renewal. |

### Channel Partners

Insurance buyers rely heavily on consultants and system integrators for technology decisions. Channel partners can accelerate distribution:

| Partner Type | Examples | Value to Charterwell | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Management consultants** | McKinsey (Insurance Practice), Deloitte (Insurance Consulting), EY (Insurance & Actuarial), PwC | Influence over carrier technology strategy. "McKinsey recommended it" is the most powerful sales asset in insurance. | Months 6-12: establish relationships. Months 12-18: formal partnerships. |
| **System integrators** | Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini, Infosys | Implementation services for enterprise deployments. SIs already implement Guidewire/Duck Creek — Charterwell becomes part of their stack. | Months 8-12: joint pilot proposals. |
| **Guidewire ecosystem** | Guidewire Marketplace partners | Direct distribution to Guidewire's 300+ carrier customers. Charterwell listed on the Guidewire Marketplace. | Months 4-8: build Guidewire integration. Months 8-12: apply for Marketplace listing. |
| **Insurance brokers / consultants** | Aon, Marsh, WTW (Willis Towers Watson) — their insurance consulting divisions | Broker consultants advise carriers on technology. Influence at the buying-committee level. | Months 12-18: relationship building. |
| **Industry analysts** | Celent, Novarica (Datos Insights), Gartner | Analyst endorsement is table stakes for enterprise consideration. Being included in a Celent or Novarica report opens doors. | Months 3-6: analyst briefings. Months 6-12: inclusion in reports. |

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Landscape Analysis

| Vendor | Pricing Model (Estimated) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Guidewire** | Subscription — per-policy or per-premium-volume basis. Multi-year enterprise agreements. Custom pricing per carrier. | $5-50M+ per deployment. The benchmark for "what carriers are willing to pay." |
| **Duck Creek** | Subscription SaaS. Cloud-hosted. | Aggressive on pricing vs. Guidewire. "90-day implementation" reduces total cost of ownership narrative. |
| **Tractable** | Per-claim or per-assessment usage-based | Aligns cost with volume. Carriers like usage-based for predictability against claim volume. |
| **Shift Technology** | Platform fee + usage-based (per-claim or per-transaction) | Combination model. Platform fee ensures minimum revenue; usage captures volume. |
| **Hyperscience** | Per-document or per-page processing | Pure usage-based. Transparent but can become expensive at scale. |
| **FurtherAI** | Unknown (early stage) | Likely custom enterprise pricing. |

### Recommended Pricing Model: Platform + Usage

**Structure:**

| Component | Description | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Platform fee** (annual) | Base fee for access to the Charterwell workspace, standard integrations, and compliance module | Ensures minimum ARR per customer. Covers infrastructure and support costs. Signals enterprise-grade commitment (not a pay-per-use tool). |
| **Usage fee** (per-claim) | Per-claim processing fee based on documents processed through the workspace | Aligns Charterwell's revenue with the carrier's claims volume. Expansion revenue as carriers add claim types and geographies. Carriers understand per-claim economics — it's how they think about cost. |
| **Playbook setup** (one-time) | Professional services fee for initial playbook configuration (adjudication rules, compliance checklists, escalation triggers) | Captures value from the institutional knowledge encoding. Creates investment that increases switching costs. |

**Illustrative Pricing (Year 1 targets):**

| Carrier Size | Platform Fee | Per-Claim Fee | Playbook Setup | Estimated ACV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mid-market** ($1-5B GWP) | $150K-300K/year | $5-15 per claim | $50K-100K | $500K-1M |
| **Large** ($5-20B GWP) | $300K-750K/year | $3-10 per claim | $100K-250K | $1M-3M |
| **Top 20** ($20B+ GWP) | $750K-2M/year | $2-8 per claim | $250K-500K | $3M-10M |

**Design partner pricing:** Free or 80% discount during pilot. Lock in Year 1 enterprise pricing at 50% of list as reward for early adoption and co-development investment.

### Why This Model Works for Insurance Buyers

1. **Predictable budgeting.** Platform fee is fixed; usage fee scales with claims volume, which carriers already forecast.
2. **Aligned incentives.** Charterwell makes more when the carrier processes more claims through the workspace — we're incentivized to drive adoption.
3. **Expansion built in.** Adding new claim types, geographies, or lines of business increases per-claim volume without renegotiating the platform fee.
4. **Comparable to existing spend.** Carriers already pay per-policy (Guidewire) and per-claim (Tractable, Shift). This model maps to existing procurement frameworks.
5. **Switching cost amplifier.** The playbook setup fee represents real investment that the carrier doesn't want to repeat with a competitor.

|---|---|---|
| **1** | Foundation | Finalize brand identity. Launch website. Begin LinkedIn content. Analyst briefing prep. Start warm intro outreach for design partners. |

## 6. Key Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Design partner pilots fail to show ROI** | Medium | Critical | Define narrow, achievable pilot scope. Pre-test on carrier's sample documents before committing. Set realistic targets (not "10x improvement" — aim for 2-3x). |
| **Shift Technology expands into document processing before we establish** | Medium | High | Speed is the mitigation. 18-24 month window. Prioritize document intelligence depth over workflow breadth. |
| **FurtherAI raises a large round and accelerates** | Medium | High | Differentiate on depth (document layer) vs. breadth (many workflows). Build playbook system early — it's our primary moat FurtherAI lacks. |
| **Enterprise sales cycles take longer than 6-12 months** | High | Medium | Over-index on design partner pipeline. Have 2x the prospects needed. Use conference relationships to pre-warm prospects 6+ months before close. |
| **Guidewire builds competitive document AI** | Low | High | Partner, don't compete. Deep Guidewire integration makes us complementary. If Guidewire acquires a document AI vendor, our integration makes us the logical acquisition target. |
| **Naming conflict with Chartwell Insurance Services** | Medium | Medium | Resolve with trademark counsel in Month 1. If conflict exists, rebrand early — before significant brand investment. See 03-brand-identity.md. |
