## 1. Brand Positioning

**Full deliverable:** `docs/marketing/01-category-creation.md`, `docs/marketing/03-brand-identity.md`

### Category

**The AI Claims Workspace** — a new category of software that gives claims professionals a unified, AI-native environment to process, analyze, and act on the documents, data, and decisions that drive every claim.

### Positioning Statement

> Charterwell is the AI Claims Workspace. Unlike point solutions that automate a single step or legacy platforms that bolt AI onto decades-old architecture, Charterwell is built from the ground up around intelligence: every document understood, every decision informed, every workflow connected.

### Differentiation

| Vs. | Our Position |
|---|---|
| Point solutions (Tractable, Shift, Sprout.ai) | One workspace replaces 10+ fragmented tools |
| Horizontal IDP (Hyperscience, ABBYY) | Insurance-intelligent, not just document extraction |
| Legacy platforms (Guidewire, Duck Creek) | AI-native intelligence layer that enhances, not replaces, core systems |
| FurtherAI | Deep on documents vs. broad but shallow across workflows |
| Build in-house | 88% want to build, 7% succeed. We deliver in 90 days what takes years. |

### Brand Voice

Professional, intelligent, confident. McKinsey meets Stripe. We speak insurance, not Silicon Valley. Specific over vague. Numbers over superlatives.

### Naming Status

"Charterwell" has strong brand qualities but proximity to "Chartwell Insurance Services" (one letter difference, same industry) is a risk. **Action required: formal trademark search with IP counsel before public launch.**

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Full deliverable:** `docs/marketing/02-buyer-personas.md`

| Persona | Role | Buying Function | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Operator** | VP/SVP Claims Operations | Primary buyer — owns budget and pain | "Cut your claims cycle time in half without adding headcount." |
| **The Champion** | Claims Team Lead / Senior Adjuster | Internal advocate — drives adoption | "Stop alt-tabbing between 12 tools." |
| **The Architect** | CIO / Chief Digital Officer | Economic buyer — controls tech spend | "From co-pilot chaos to clear AI ROI." |
| **The Gatekeeper** | Chief Compliance Officer | Blocker — can kill with one "no" | "Full audit trail on every AI decision." |

**Typical deal flow:** Champion discovers → advocates to Operator → Operator requests pilot budget from Architect → Architect approves → pilot runs → success → Gatekeeper reviews compliance → enterprise deal closes.

**Deal timeline:** 6-12 months from first contact to enterprise contract.

## 3. GTM Plan

**Full deliverable:** `docs/marketing/04-gtm-motion.md`

### Motion

```
Design Partners (Months 1-4) → Proof of Value (Months 4-8) → Enterprise Conversion (Months 8-12)
```

### Design Partner Targets

- **Profile:** Mid-market P&C carrier, $1-10B GWP, Guidewire/Duck Creek, active AI posture
- **Pipeline target:** 10 qualified conversations → 5 pilots → 2-3 paid conversions
- **Outreach channels:** Warm intros (investors/advisors), conferences, LinkedIn, content inbound, analyst introductions

### Pilot Framework

- **Duration:** 60-90 days on one claim type in one geography
- **Key success metrics:** ≥50% triage time reduction, ≥95% extraction accuracy, ≥20% cycle time reduction
- **Conversion path:** Pilot → Phase 1 expansion (more claim types) → Phase 2 (all geographies) → Enterprise contract

### Pricing Model

**Platform fee + per-claim usage + playbook setup**

| Carrier Size | Estimated ACV |
|---|---|
| Mid-market ($1-5B GWP) | $500K-1M |
| Large ($5-20B GWP) | $1-3M |
| Top 20 ($20B+ GWP) | $3-10M |

Design partners: 50% of list pricing locked in for early adoption.

### Channel Partners (Months 6-18)

- **Influence:** McKinsey, Deloitte, EY, PwC insurance practices
- **Implementation:** Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini
- **Distribution:** Guidewire Marketplace
- **Validation:** Celent, Novarica analyst reports

## 5. Metrics Framework

### Pre-Revenue Marketing Metrics

These are the metrics that matter before we have paying customers. They measure whether the category narrative is landing, the pipeline is building, and the brand is gaining recognition.

#### Pipeline Metrics

| Metric | Definition | 6-Month Target | 12-Month Target | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Qualified conversations** | Prospects who've had a substantive conversation about Charterwell with CEO/CMO | 10 | 25 | CRM |
| **Design partner pipeline** | Prospects actively evaluating or in pilot | 5 | 8 | CRM |
| **Pilots active** | Design partners with live Charterwell deployments | 2-3 | 5 | Internal tracking |
| **Paid conversions** | Pilots that convert to enterprise contracts | 0 | 2-3 | CRM |
| **Pipeline value** | Total ACV of active opportunities | $2M | $5M | CRM |

#### Content & Engagement Metrics

| Metric | Definition | 6-Month Target | 12-Month Target | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Website monthly visitors** | Unique visitors to charterwell.ai | 5,000 | 15,000 | Google Analytics |
| **Whitepaper downloads** | Gated content downloads (email captured) | 200 | 600 | Website analytics |
| **Blog engagement** | Avg. time on page, scroll depth, shares | Top quartile engagement (>3 min avg.) | Maintain | Google Analytics |
| **Email subscribers** | Opted-in contacts from content + conferences | 500 | 1,500 | Email platform |
| **Content-sourced leads** | Qualified conversations originating from content | 5 | 15 | CRM attribution |

#### Conference Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target per Event | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conversations** | Meaningful meetings at or around conference | 20+ (ITC), 10+ (other) | CRM |
| **Follow-up rate** | Conversations that result in scheduled follow-up call | >50% | CRM |
| **Pipeline generated** | New qualified opportunities sourced from conference | 3-5 per major event | CRM |
| **Speaking slots** | Accepted speaking engagements | 1-2 in Year 1 | Manual |

#### Brand Awareness Metrics

| Metric | Definition | 6-Month Target | 12-Month Target | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn followers** (company) | Charterwell company page followers | 1,000 | 3,000 | LinkedIn |
| **LinkedIn engagement rate** | Avg. engagement on company + founder posts | >2% | >2.5% | LinkedIn analytics |
| **Share of voice** | Charterwell mentions in insurance AI conversations (LinkedIn, publications, conferences) | Mentioned in 2+ industry publications | Mentioned in 5+ publications + 1 analyst report | Media monitoring |
| **"AI Claims Workspace" search ranking** | Google ranking for category-defining keyword | Page 1 | Position 1-3 | SEMrush/Ahrefs |
| **Analyst recognition** | Inclusion in Celent, Novarica, or Gartner research | 1 briefing completed | 1 report inclusion | Manual |
| **Inbound carrier interest** | Unprompted inquiries from carriers who found Charterwell through content, search, or referral | 3 | 10+ | CRM |

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

| Type | Metrics | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Leading (track weekly)** | LinkedIn engagement, website traffic, content output cadence, outreach volume | These predict future pipeline. If they drop, pipeline dries up in 60-90 days. |
| **Coincident (track monthly)** | Qualified conversations, whitepaper downloads, conference meetings, email list growth | These show current momentum. Are prospects engaging with us right now? |
| **Lagging (track quarterly)** | Pilots active, paid conversions, analyst recognition, inbound carrier interest, revenue | These confirm strategy is working. If leading indicators are strong but lagging ones aren't, the conversion funnel has a problem. |

### The One Metric That Matters Most

**Inbound interest from carriers.**

Before we have revenue, before we have press, the single most important signal is: **are carriers finding us and reaching out unprompted?** This means:
- The category narrative is resonating
- The content is reaching the right people
- The positioning is differentiated enough to generate curiosity
- The brand has enough credibility to warrant a conversation

If carriers are reaching out to us, category creation is working. If we're only generating interest through outbound, the category narrative needs refinement.

**6-month target: 3 unprompted carrier inquiries.** If we hit this, the strategy is working. If we don't, we need to diagnose whether the problem is reach (not enough people seeing our content), resonance (content isn't compelling), or relevance (targeting the wrong audience).

## 7. Key Risks & Dependencies

| Risk | Mitigation | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Chartwell/Charterwell naming conflict | Formal trademark search — Month 1 | CEO + Legal |
| Design partner pilots fail to show ROI | Narrow pilot scope, pre-test on sample docs, realistic targets | CEO + Product |
| Shift Technology or FurtherAI moves faster | Speed to market. Deep document intelligence. Playbook system as moat. | Product + Engineering |
| Enterprise sales cycles exceed 12 months | 2x pipeline. Over-index on warm intros. Pre-warm prospects at conferences 6+ months out. | CEO + CMO |
| Content doesn't generate inbound interest | Test messaging in LinkedIn posts before investing in long-form. A/B test topics. Talk to prospects about what resonates. | CMO |
