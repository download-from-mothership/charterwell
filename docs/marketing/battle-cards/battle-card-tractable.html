CHARTERWELL Competitive Brief
Tractable Computer Vision • $185M raised • $1B valuation
Who They Are

Computer vision platform for auto and property damage appraisal from photos. AI-powered visual inspection used by insurers globally.

What They Do Well
  • Best-in-class auto/property damage assessment from photos
  • Marquee carriers: Tokio Marine, The Hartford, Ageas
  • Global footprint — 10+ countries, proven at scale
Where They Fall Short
  • Photos only — zero document intelligence capability
  • Cannot process policy text, medical records, compliance filings, or adjuster notes
  • Single use case (damage appraisal) in a multi-document workflow
  • Covers <15% of the document surface area of an average P&C claim
Our Counter-Position

"Tractable reads photos. Charterwell reads everything else. A claim isn't a photo — it's 10–15 documents. We start where Tractable stops."

Proof Points
  • Average P&C claim touches 10–15 documents; only 1–2 are images
  • Tractable covers <15% of document surface area in a typical claim
  • Claims decisions depend on policy language, medical records, and compliance — none of which are photos
Trap Questions
  • "How do you handle the police report, medical records, and policy declaration page?"
  • "What happens after the damage estimate — who routes the claim, checks coverage, ensures compliance?"
  • "How many document types does a typical claim involve beyond photos?"
Partnership Angle

Tractable is a natural integration partner. Their damage scores feed directly into the Charterwell workspace. Complementary, not competitive — position accordingly.
