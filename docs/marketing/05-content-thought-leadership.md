# Charterwell Content & Thought Leadership Strategy

**Prepared by:** CMO | **Date:** 2026-03-21 | **Issue:** KEN-40

---

## Executive Summary

Content is the engine of category creation. Before we have product-market fit, before we have case studies, before we have revenue — we need to own the conversation about AI in insurance claims. This document defines the content strategy that establishes Charterwell as the definitive voice on the AI Claims Workspace category.

The strategy has four pillars: founding content (the manifesto), research & thought leadership (the credibility), conference presence (the relationships), and digital presence (the reach).

---

## 1. Founding Content: The Manifesto

### "The Claims Floor is Broken. AI Won't Fix It. A Workspace Will."

This is our category-defining piece. It introduces the AI Claims Workspace concept, explains why point solutions and legacy platforms can't solve the problem, and positions Charterwell as the answer. The narrative already exists in 01-category-creation.md (Section 4) — it needs to be refined into a publishable format.

### Specifications

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Format** | Long-form whitepaper (3,000-4,000 words) + condensed blog post (1,200 words) |
| **Target audience** | VP Claims, CIO, industry analysts |
| **Tone** | Thought leadership — authoritative, evidence-based, opinionated but not promotional |
| **Key argument** | The insurance AI market is fragmented between point solutions and legacy platforms. No one has built the workspace. Here's why that matters, and what it looks like. |
| **CTA** | "Join the design partner program" / "Schedule a conversation" |
| **Distribution** | Website (gated for whitepaper, ungated for blog), LinkedIn, email to prospect list, analyst briefing leave-behind |
| **Timeline** | Draft: Month 1. Published: Month 2. |

### Outline

1. **The promise and the gap.** 88% of carriers deploying AI. 7% scaling it. Why?
2. **Trap 1: Point solution fatigue.** 17 data sources, 10+ tools, BCG's "siloed use cases" finding.
3. **Trap 2: Legacy architecture ceiling.** Guidewire/Duck Creek AI is bolted on, not built in.
4. **The workspace model.** How Legora proved it in legal. How Figma proved it in design. How Bloomberg proved it in finance.
5. **What the AI Claims Workspace looks like.** Intake → Extraction → Routing → Adjudication → Compliance → Payout. Connected, not siloed.
6. **Why now.** AI capability threshold crossed. Workforce crisis accelerating. Regulatory pressure increasing.
7. **The invitation.** Join the carriers building the next generation of claims operations.

## 5. Content Production Workflow

### Roles

| Role | Responsibility | Who (Year 1) |
|---|---|---|
| **Content strategist** | Calendar, briefs, editorial direction | CMO |
| **Writer** | Draft blogs, whitepapers, reports | CMO + freelance insurance technology writer |
| **Subject matter expert** | Technical accuracy, product details | CEO / Founding Engineer |
| **Designer** | Report layouts, social graphics, infographics | Contract designer |
| **Editor** | Final review for voice consistency and accuracy | CMO |

### Process

```
Brief (CMO) → Draft (Writer) → Expert Review (CEO/Eng) → Edit (CMO) → Design → Publish
```

**Turnaround targets:**
- Blog posts: 5 business days from brief to publish
- Whitepapers: 10 business days
- Research reports: 15-20 business days

### Quality Bar

Every published piece must pass:

1. **The "so what" test.** Does this say something that matters to a VP Claims? If it's generic AI content with "insurance" swapped in, kill it.
2. **The evidence test.** Is every claim supported by data, a named source, or a specific example? No hand-waving.
3. **The voice test.** Does it sound like Charterwell? (See 03-brand-identity.md, Section 1.)
4. **The category test.** Does it reinforce the AI Claims Workspace narrative? Every piece of content should build the category, even obliquely.
