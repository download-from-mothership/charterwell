# Charterwell Burn Rate & Runway Analysis

**Prepared by:** CFO | **Date:** 2026-03-21 | **Issue:** KEN-29

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis models three scenarios (conservative, base, aggressive) for Charterwell's monthly burn rate, cash runway, and revenue ramp across the first 24 months post-seed. All scenarios assume a **$12M seed raise**.

**Key finding:** Under all three scenarios, Charterwell has sufficient runway to reach Series A without a bridge round. The "danger zone" begins at **Month 26 (conservative)**, **Month 30 (base)**, or **Month 22 (aggressive)** — the point at which the company must either demonstrate Series A metrics or begin cutting burn.

## 4. The Danger Zone

The "danger zone" is when Charterwell must either (a) demonstrate Series A metrics, (b) secure a bridge round, or (c) cut burn to extend runway. We define it as **<12 months of runway remaining at current net burn rate without factoring in revenue growth.**

### When Does the Danger Zone Hit?

| Scenario | Danger Zone Begins | Cash at Danger Zone | What Must Be True |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** | **Month 26** | ~$6.5M | ARR must be >$1M or cut 4 headcount to extend 6 months |
| **Base** | **Month 30+** | ~$7M | ARR >$2M and Series A process underway |
| **Aggressive** | **Never** (profitable at M20) | N/A | Cash-flow positive before danger zone |

### Decision Tree at the Danger Zone

```
Month 20 Assessment:
├── ARR > $2M, pipeline 3x → Proceed to Series A (target close M22–24)
├── ARR $1–2M, pipeline 2x → Extend runway: freeze hiring, cut non-essential opex
│   └── Buys 6–8 additional months for metrics to mature
├── ARR < $1M, weak pipeline → Emergency mode:
│   ├── Option A: Bridge round ($3–5M from existing investors)
│   ├── Option B: RIF to 10 people, cut burn to $180K/mo → 24+ months runway
│   └── Option C: Pivot or acqui-hire conversations
└── Cash-flow positive → Skip Series A or raise from position of strength
```

### Burn Cut Playbook (If Needed)

| Action | Monthly Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Freeze hiring at 14 people (vs. 18 plan) | $60K/mo | Delays product roadmap by ~6 months |
| Cut marketing to minimum | $12K/mo | Slows pipeline; acceptable if product-led |
| Renegotiate cloud contracts | $5K/mo | Minimal impact |
| Move to fully remote (close office) | $10K/mo | May affect recruiting |
| Reduce founder salaries 20% | $6K/mo | Signal commitment to investors |
| **Total emergency cut** | **$93K/mo** | Extends runway by ~8 months at M20 cash levels |

### Key Insight: The Base Case Is Very Comfortable

Under the base case:
- Cash never drops below **$7.9M** in the first 24 months
- Net burn **peaks at Month 12** and declines thereafter as revenue scales
- By Month 24, the company is burning only **$124K/mo net** — essentially self-sustaining
- Series A can be raised from a position of **strength, not desperation**

Even the conservative case has 38 months of runway at M24 — enough time to iterate on product-market fit without existential pressure.

## 6. Key Assumptions & Sensitivity

| Assumption | Conservative | Base | Aggressive | Impact if Wrong |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seed raise | $12M | $12M | $12M | ±$3M shifts danger zone by ±6 months |
| Avg. loaded cost/employee | $17.7K/mo | $17.7K/mo | $17.9K/mo | +10% = ~$5K/mo at M6, $32K/mo at M24 |
| Revenue start | M8 | M4 | M3 | Each month delay costs ~$25K in missed revenue |
| Monthly infra at M24 | $22K | $31K | $45K | Low sensitivity — <10% of total burn |
| LLM cost per claim | $0.09 | $0.09 | $0.09 | Even 5x increase ($0.45) adds only $35K/mo at 80K claims |
| Office cost | $5–15K/mo | $5–15K/mo | $5–15K/mo | Fully cuttable in emergency |

### What Would Need to Be True for Each Scenario

**Conservative (things go wrong):**
- Sales cycle is 12+ months (regulatory delays, budget freezes)
- Design partners churn or don't convert
- Only 1 new customer per quarter
- LLM costs don't decline as expected

**Base (plan execution):**
- 2 design partners convert by M6
- Sales pipeline generates 2 qualified leads per quarter by M8
- NRR hits 115% by M12
- Hiring plan executes on schedule

**Aggressive (breakout):**
- Product achieves viral adoption among adjusters (bottom-up adoption within carriers)
- Sales cycle compresses to 6 months due to clear ROI demonstration
- Multi-carrier pilot program drives 3+ simultaneous closes
- Strong word-of-mouth reduces CAC below $75K
