**Prepared by:** CFO | **Date:** 2026-03-21 | **Issue:** KEN-26

## Executive Summary

This analysis benchmarks Charterwell against five comparable companies spanning vertical AI (legal, insurance) and incumbent insurance software. The comps support a seed-stage narrative of a large, proven market with AI-native companies commanding premium multiples (25-60x ARR), while the insurance vertical remains under-penetrated relative to legal AI.

## Detailed Company Profiles

### Legora — The Closest Vertical AI Analog

- **Founded:** Sweden, ~2022
- **Customers:** ~800 law firms and legal teams
- **Avg. contract:** ~$280K/year → **implied ARR: ~$224M**
- **Employees:** 400; growing to 300+ in U.S. alone by EOY 2026
- **Fundraising cadence:**

| Round | Amount | Valuation | Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seed | Undisclosed | — | — |
| Series A | Undisclosed | — | — |
| Series B | $80M | $675M | ~2024 |
| Series C | $150M | $1.8B | Oct 2025 |
| Series D | $550M | $5.55B | Mar 2026 |

- **Key insight:** Valuation tripled from $1.8B to $5.55B in <6 months (Series C → D). This was driven by rapid U.S. expansion, customer count scaling, and the broader AI legal-tech market tailwind. Legora's trajectory shows that vertical AI companies with strong product-market fit can compress fundraising timelines dramatically.
- **Implied ARR multiple:** ~25x at Series D — below Harvey but reflects a more capital-efficient growth path ($816M raised vs. Harvey's $1B+).

### Harvey AI — The Valuation Ceiling for Vertical AI

- **Founded:** San Francisco, 2022
- **ARR:** $190M (EOY 2025), up from $100M (Aug 2025) — nearly 2x in ~4 months
- **Fundraising cadence:**

| Round | Amount | Valuation | Date | Lead |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Series D | $300M | $3B | Feb 2025 | Sequoia |
| Series E | $300M | $5B | Jun 2025 | Kleiner Perkins / Coatue |
| Series F | $160M | $8B | Dec 2025 | a16z |
| Series G (reported) | $200M | $11B | Feb 2026 | Sequoia / GIC |

- **Key insight:** Harvey's multiple expansion (42x → 58x implied) is driven by hypergrowth and perceived winner-take-most dynamics in legal AI. Four rounds in 14 months is extraordinary. The $11B target represents a ~58x forward multiple — aggressive even for AI, suggesting investors are pricing in $300M+ ARR within 12 months.
- **Relevance to Charterwell:** Harvey proves that vertical AI for professional services commands massive premiums. Insurance is an equally large market with similar AI adoption dynamics.

### Tractable — Insurance AI Pure-Play

- **Founded:** London, 2014
- **Total raised:** $185M across 7 rounds
- **Last known valuation:** $1B (Series D, Jun 2021; refreshed with $65M Series E in Jul 2023)
- **Product:** AI-powered visual damage assessment for auto and property insurance claims
- **Key investors:** Insight Partners, Georgian, SoftBank Vision Fund 2
- **Key insight:** Tractable is the most direct insurance AI comp. The $1B valuation on $185M raised suggests strong capital efficiency. However, the lack of a public funding event since mid-2023 raises questions about growth trajectory. No disclosed ARR makes multiple comparison difficult. Product scope is narrower than Charterwell's vision (visual appraisal only vs. full claims intelligence).

### Shift Technology — Insurance AI (Acquired)

- **Founded:** Paris, 2014
- **Total raised:** $316M
- **Peak valuation:** $1B+ (Series D, May 2021)
- **Product:** AI-powered fraud detection, claims handling, underwriting decision support
- **Outcome:** Acquired by Predictiv AI (closed Dec 2025)
- **Key insight:** Shift's acquisition validates the insurance AI category but at a modest outcome relative to fundraising ($316M raised, acquired at undisclosed but likely sub-$1B given acquirer profile). The five-year AXA renewal (Mar 2026) shows enterprise stickiness. Shift's cautionary lesson: fraud detection alone may not be a large enough wedge — Charterwell's broader claims intelligence positioning is stronger.

### Guidewire (GWRE) — The Incumbent to Displace

- **Founded:** 2001 | **Public since:** 2012
- **Market cap:** $13.6B (Mar 2026)
- **FY2025 revenue:** $1,202M (+23% YoY)
- **ARR:** $1,032M (Jul 2025, +19% YoY)
- **FY2026 guidance:** $1.44-1.45B revenue
- **Revenue multiple:** 11.3x (market cap / ARR); ~9.5x (market cap / FY2026E revenue)
- **Key insight:** Guidewire is the gold-standard P&C insurance platform. Its $13.6B market cap on ~$1B ARR (11x) represents the mature-company multiple. AI-native challengers like Charterwell would command a significant premium at growth stage (25-50x+ based on Legora/Harvey comps). Guidewire's 23% revenue growth shows the market is expanding, but their legacy architecture creates an opening for AI-first entrants.

## Valuation Implications for Charterwell

### What the comps tell us:

| Metric | Range from Comps | Commentary |
|---|---|---|
| Seed valuation | $30-60M pre-money | Standard for vertical AI with strong founding team, pre-revenue |
| Series A trigger ARR | $1-3M | Based on Legora/Harvey trajectory at comparable stages |
| Series A multiple | 80-150x ARR | Early-stage premium for high-growth vertical AI |
| Steady-state multiple (Series B+) | 25-60x ARR | Based on Legora ($25x) and Harvey ($42-58x) |
| TAM anchor | $13.6B+ | Guidewire market cap alone; broader insurance software TAM is $30B+ |

### Key takeaways for fundraising narrative:

1. **Legal AI validates the playbook.** Legora and Harvey prove that vertical AI for regulated professional services commands 25-60x ARR multiples. Insurance is an analogous market with similar complexity, regulatory burden, and willingness to pay.

2. **Insurance AI is under-capitalized.** Tractable ($185M) and Shift ($316M) raised a fraction of what legal AI peers have raised. No insurance AI company has broken out to $5B+ valuation yet — Charterwell has an open lane.

3. **The incumbent is vulnerable but valuable.** Guidewire's $13.6B market cap proves the market size. Their cloud transition (23% revenue growth) shows demand, but an AI-native architecture can leapfrog legacy systems.

4. **Speed matters.** Legora tripled its valuation in 5 months. Harvey raised 4 rounds in 14 months. In vertical AI, the window to establish category leadership is narrow — Charterwell should raise aggressively once product-market fit is demonstrated.

5. **Target seed raise: $10-15M** at a $40-60M pre-money valuation is well-supported by these comps, assuming a strong founding team and working product with early design partners.
