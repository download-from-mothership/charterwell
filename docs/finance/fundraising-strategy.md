# Charterwell Fundraising Strategy

**Prepared by:** CFO | **Date:** 2026-03-21 | **Issue:** KEN-28

---

## Executive Summary

This document lays out Charterwell's fundraising roadmap from seed through Series A, including milestones that justify each raise, target metrics for Series A readiness, and a ranked investor target list of 30 firms across three priority tiers.

**Bottom line:** Raise a $12M seed at $50M pre-money now (Q2 2026), target Series A of $30–50M at $200–300M pre-money in Q4 2027 – Q2 2028 once $2–3M ARR is demonstrated.

---

## 1. Seed Round: $10–15M (Target: $12M)

### Why $12M?

| Factor | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **24-month runway** | At projected burn, $12M funds 28+ months to Series A — no bridge needed |
| **Team of 18** | Enough to build product, close 10 enterprise customers, and hit Series A metrics |
| **Market signal** | Legora raised at $5.55B, Harvey at $8–11B — vertical AI is the hottest category in VC. Investors want meaningful allocation |
| **Comp-supported** | Sixfold (insurance AI) raised $6.5M seed from BVP. $12M is aggressive but justified by larger TAM ambition |

### Target Terms

| Term | Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-money valuation | $40–60M | 25–50x on forward ARR projections; aligned with vertical AI seed comps |
| Dilution | 17–23% | Standard for $10–15M seed |
| Structure | Priced equity (Series Seed Preferred) | Cleaner than SAFE at this size; investors prefer it |
| Board seats | 1 investor seat + 1 independent | Maintain founder control |
| Pro-rata rights | Standard for lead investor | Expected |

### Milestones That Justify This Raise

Investors at seed will evaluate Charterwell on team, market, and early signals — not revenue. The following milestones make the case:

1. **Working product** with demonstrated AI accuracy on real insurance claims data (>90% categorization accuracy, >80% summary quality score)
2. **2–3 design partners** — named P&C carriers actively testing the product (LOIs or pilot agreements in hand)
3. **Founding team credibility** — relevant domain expertise in insurance + AI/ML
4. **Market timing** — insurance AI is under-capitalized vs. legal AI ($500M total raised vs. $2B+), with Guidewire's $13.6B market cap proving TAM
5. **Data moat thesis** — every claim processed improves the model; first-mover advantage compounds

## 2. Series A Triggers (12–18 Months Post-Seed)

### Must-Hit Metrics for Series A

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **ARR** | $2–3M | Proves repeatable revenue; aligns with vertical AI Series A benchmarks |
| **Customers** | 6–10 enterprise logos | Shows breadth beyond design partners |
| **NRR** | >120% | Proves expansion motion (usage growth + tier upgrades) |
| **Gross margin** | >75% | Validates that LLM costs don't erode margins |
| **Logo retention** | >90% annually | Enterprise stickiness |
| **Claims processed** | >50K/month | Scale signal; feeds the data moat narrative |
| **Sales pipeline** | 3x ARR target | Demonstrates forward momentum |
| **Burn multiple** | <3x | Capital efficiency signal (net new ARR / net burn) |

### Series A Target Terms

| Term | Target |
|---|---|
| Raise amount | $30–50M |
| Pre-money valuation | $200–300M (80–120x ARR at $2.5M) |
| Dilution | 12–18% |
| Lead investor profile | Tier 1 enterprise/vertical AI fund |

**Valuation justification:** Legora trades at 25x ARR at Series D ($5.55B on ~$224M ARR). Harvey at 42–58x. Early-stage vertical AI commands 80–150x at Series A due to growth premium. $200–300M on $2.5M ARR = 80–120x, well within range.

| **Q2 2026** | Close seed round |
| **Q3–Q4 2026** | Product build, design partner onboarding |
| **Q1 2027** | First paying customers; begin tracking ARR |

## 4. Outreach Strategy

### Sequencing (Do Not Spray and Pray)

| Phase | Timing | Firms | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Warm-up** | Week 1–2 | 3–4 Tier 2 insurtech CVCs | Get term sheets or strong interest to create urgency |
| **Phase 2: Tier 1 blitz** | Week 3–5 | 5–6 Tier 1 vertical AI leads | Run a compressed process; use Tier 2 interest as leverage |
| **Phase 3: Fill & close** | Week 5–7 | Remaining firms for co-invest allocation | Fill the round with strategic value-adds |

### Why This Sequencing?

- **Tier 2 first** creates social proof and urgency without "burning" your best investors. Insurtech CVCs move faster (fewer layers) and bring strategic value regardless.
- **Tier 1 blitz** compresses decision timelines. These firms are competitive with each other (Accel vs. Sequoia vs. a16z); run them in parallel.
- **Never fundraise for more than 8 weeks.** If you don't have a term sheet by Week 6, pause and diagnose (product, metrics, or narrative problem).

### Key Narrative Points for Each Tier

| Audience | Lead With | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (Vertical AI)** | "We're doing for insurance what Harvey/Legora did for legal — but insurance is 3x the market and 0.1x the AI investment" | Over-emphasizing insurance complexity; they know AI, show them the market |
| **Tier 2 (Insurtech)** | "AI-native claims intelligence replacing Guidewire-era workflows — your portfolio carriers need this" | Abstract AI hype; ground it in claims processing ROI and carrier pain points |
| **Tier 3 (Generalists)** | "Vertical AI with a data moat in a $30B+ market — first mover with the highest ACV vertical left" | Insurance jargon; keep it as a platform/AI story |

## 5. Competitive Fundraising Landscape

### Capital Gap: Insurance AI vs. Legal AI

| Metric | Legal AI | Insurance AI | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total VC raised (top 2 cos) | $1.8B (Harvey + Legora) | $500M (Tractable + Shift) | Insurance AI is 3.6x under-capitalized |
| Highest valuation | $11B (Harvey) | $1B (Tractable, 2021) | No breakout insurance AI company yet |
| Market size (incumbent proxy) | N/A | $13.6B (Guidewire alone) | Proven, massive TAM |
| AI adoption rate | High (law firms racing to adopt) | Moderate but accelerating (76% of US insurers using genAI) | Timing is right — early majority adoption phase |

**This is the core pitch:** The vertical AI playbook is proven (legal). Insurance is a larger market with less competition and less capital deployed. Charterwell is the first AI-native claims intelligence platform positioned to be the "Harvey of insurance."
